Skala Kallonis: A Tourism Hotspot

Located at the heart of Lesvos, Skala Kallonis sits beside one of Europe's most important wetlands. Its booming summer tourism generates a hidden cost — organic food waste that currently goes unrecovered.

🐟

The Sardine Economy

World-renowned "Papalina" sardine festivals each August draw thousands of visitors, generating concentrated fish-waste streams from ~25 waterfront tavernas.

♨️

~2.28 kg

🦩

Natura 2000 Zone

The Gulf of Kalloni wetland hosts 311 bird species including flamingos. Any energy solution must safeguard this ecological asset.

⚡

Energy Isolation

As a Greek island, Lesvos relies on expensive grid imports. Energy self-sufficiency is both economically and strategically vital.

Why Biogas? Anaerobic digestion of food waste produces ~261 litres of biogas per kg of cooked food waste. Run through a CHP unit, 1 m³ of biogas yields 1.2–1.8 kWh of electricity — sufficient to power a household's daily lighting and basic appliances.

System Specification

Covered lagoon / CSTR (Continuous Stirred Tank Reactor) — 60–80 m³ working volume. Mesophilic operation at 35–38°C, enabled by waste heat from CHP unit. Hydraulic Retention Time (HRT): 20–30 days. A double-digester configuration (2 × 40 m³) allows maintenance without system shutdown.
Low-pressure gas bag storage (10–20 m³). H₂S removal via iron scrubber (essential for engine longevity). CO₂ remains — no upgrading required for CHP. Moisture separation via condensate trap. Estimated gas pressure: 10–20 mbar.
Modified internal combustion generator, 1–3 kW electrical output, running on 100% biogas. At 20–30% electrical efficiency: 1 m³ biogas → 1.2–1.8 kWh electricity. With 48,200 m³/year feedstock potential and conservative 60% utilisation: ~34,000–52,000 kWh/year electrical generation.
Approximately 70–80% of feedstock becomes liquid digestate — a nitrogen-rich biofertiliser. Estimated output: 130–170 tons/year. Applied to Kalloni valley olive orchards and vegetable plots. Eliminates chemical fertiliser cost (~€80–120/ton equivalent).
Under Greek Law 5037/2023 (REC framework) and Law 5215/2025, RECs can apply virtual net metering (VNM) — surplus electricity credited against member bills. Connection to DEDDIE (island distribution network). Installed capacity likely <500 kW, falling under simplified licensing regime.
Climate Advantage Lesvos's Mediterranean climate (average 18°C annual) reduces digester heating requirements relative to northern Europe, improving year-round biogas yield consistency.

Collection Logistics & Waste Supply Chain

The compact geography of Skala Kallonis — a linear harbour village of roughly 1.5 km — makes waste collection extremely efficient. All major waste generators are within a single route.

🚛

Collection Route Design

Single daily cargo vehicle (≤3.5 ton) with refrigerated compartment for food waste. Collection windows: 06:00–08:00 (restaurants) and 09:00–10:00 (households).

📦

Collection Infrastructure

25-litre HDPE bio-bins at each collection point. 120-litre shared bins per 3–4 households. Sealed to prevent odour and animal disturbance.

🌿

Digestate Distribution

Liquid digestate returned to Kalloni valley agricultural plots. Digestate given to local farmers as biofertiliser.

Winter Gap (Nov–Mar) Restaurant waste drops ~85% in off-season. Mitigation: co-digest with livestock manure from Kalloni valley farms and olive mill wastewater (November crush season). Blending maintains digester stability through winter.
